Mount modules in an rack is kind of difficult. Find the right rack and mounting setup is not that easy. or is it? Lets find out in this article on how to construct a simple modular synthesizer system.

International standard 19” rack system (DIN 41494 / IEC 297-3 / IEEE 1001.1). Wwhen modules are built, they have to be mounted somewhere. the de facto standard is the eurorack format. the eurorack standard is derived from the 19-inch rack standard. this standard defines all the parts needed to build a complete rack. i am only using the rails to mount the modules. the rails will be screwed to a wooden side panel. you will read about that in a later article. in this article the pcb and panel sizes are described.

pcb size

panel mounting holes

the panel mount holes are placed 7.5mm from the left and right side of the panel and 5mm from the top and buttom. the resulting must be in steps of one inch or 2.54mm. One inch is the spacing of the screw holes on the mounting rail. there is alway one mounting hole on the left of the panel. On the right side there is one if the panel is equal or larger then 8 HP. the holes dimmension is M3.

mount holes for stacking

the mounting holes for stacking the main and mount pcb are 1 inch or 2.54mm from the side. modules are only stackable if the width is equal or larger then 8HP. the holes dimmension is M2.
